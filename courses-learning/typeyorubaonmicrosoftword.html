Yorùbá on Microsoft Word

In this Lesson, you will learn how to type Yoruba words using Microsoft Word


NB!
Yoruba has three tones: high, low, and mid. Only high and low are usually marked in the orthography. The mid is left unmarked. So if you see any unmarked vowel in any Standard Yoruba write-up, it simply carries a mid-tone.

1. TONES


High-tone = acute symbol ( ′ )


to type, just press
Ctrl + ʻ followed by
the vowel (you want to tone-mark), e.g. e, o, u, A
This will give you é, ó, ú, Á


Low-tone = grave symbol ( ` )


to type, just press
Ctrl + ` followed by
the vowel (you want to tone-mark), e.g. e, o, u, A
This will give you è, ò, ù, ì, À

Mid-tone = has no symbol




2. SUB - DOT


NB!
The other orthographic modification used in Yoruba is the sub-dot.
This is placed under vowels e, o, and consonant s to indicate they are pronounced as;
ẹ /ɛ/ as e in English let
ọ /ɔ/ as aw in English saw
ṣ /∫/ as sh in English should

to insert sub-dot under any vowel:
put the cursor immediately after the vowel (without space)
go to symbol and choose appropriate dot symbol
(see demonstration)
select/press to insert, e.g. ẹ, ọ, ọ́, ẹ̀, ṣ, Ṣ, Ẹ
(see demonstration)
assign a personalised shortcut key to the dot on your computer so that you won't have be repeating the process all the time.
(see demonstration on how to do this)



To tone mark syllabic nasals like n or m either for low-tone or high-tone


go to symbol and choose appropriate already tone-marked ń, ǹ, Ń, Ǹ symbol (see demonstration)
select/press to insert
go to symbol and choose appropriate dot symbol
(see demonstration)
assign a personalised shortcut key to the nasal on your computer so that you won't have be repeating the process all the time.
(see demonstration)
