1. Time of Day Based Greetings

Àárọ̀
Morning

Convers I:
Ìkíni Láàrin Akin àti ìyá Rẹ̀
Greetings between Akin and His Mother
Akin: Ẹ káàárọ̀ o, màmá Good morning, Mummy
Mummy: Káàárọ̀, Akin
Ṣé dáadáa la jí?		 Good morning, Akin
Trust you woke up well?
Akin: A dúpẹ́ Thank you.
Convers II:
Ìkíni láàrin ọ̀rẹ́ méjì, Ronkẹ́ àti Ṣadé.
Greetings between two friends, Ronke and Sade.
Ronkẹ́: Káàárọ̀ o Ṣadé. Good morning Ṣade.
Ṣadé: Káàárọ̀ o Rónkẹ́. Good morning Ronke
Ronkẹ́: Ṣé dáadáa ni? How are you?
Ṣadé: Dáadáa ni, a dúpẹ́ Fine, thank you.
Ronkẹ́: Ilé ńkọ́? Hope your family is fine?
Ṣadé: A dúpẹ́, àlàáfíà ni. We are fine, thank you.
Ronkẹ́: Káàárọ̀ o Ṣadé. Good morning Ṣade.
Ṣadé: Káàárọ̀ o Rónkẹ́. Good morning Ronke
Ronkẹ́: Ṣé dáadáa ni o? How are you?
Ṣadé: Dáadáa ni o, a dúpẹ́ Fine, thank you.
Ronkẹ́: Gbogbo Ilé ńkọ́ o? Hope your family is fine?
Ṣadé: A dúpẹ́ o, àlàáfíà la wà. We are fine, thank you.
Convers III:
Ìkíni láàrin aládùúgbò
Greetings between neighbours
Aládùúgbò 1: Ẹ káàárọ̀ o. Good morning.
Aládùúgbò 2: Ooo! Ẹ káàárọ̀. Yes! Good morning
Aládùúgbò 1: Ṣé dáadáa la jí o? Trust you woke up well?
Aládùúgbò 2: A dúpẹ́ o Thank you.
Aládùúgbò 1: Àwọn ọmọ ńkọ́ o? How are your children?
Aládùúgbò 2: Àlàáfíà ni o. They are fine. Thank you.
Aládùúgbò 1: Ẹ káàárọ̀ o. Good morning
Aládùúgbò 2: Ooo! Ẹ káàárọ̀ o Good morning
Aládùúgbò 1: Ṣé dáadáa la jí o? Trust you woke up well?
Aládùúgbò 2: A dúpẹ́ o. Thank you.
Aládùúgbò 1: Àwọn ọmọ ńkọ́ o? How is your child?
Aládùúgbò 2: Àlàáfíà ni o. Ẹ ṣé. Fine, thanks.
Aládùúgbò 1: Ẹ káàárọ̀. Good morning.
Aládùúgbò 2: Ẹ káàárọ̀. Good morning
Aládùúgbò 1: Ṣé dáadáa la jí? Trust you woke up well?
Aládùúgbò 2: A dúpẹ́. Thank you.
Aládùúgbò 1: Àwọn ọmọ ńkọ́ o? How are your children?
Aládùúgbò 2: Dáadáa ni. Ẹ ṣé. Fine, thanks.

Ọ̀sán
Afternoon

Convers IV:
Ìkíni láàrin Olùkọ́ àti Akẹ́kọ̀ọ́
Greetings between Teacher and Pupils
Àwọn akẹ́kọ̀ọ́ dìde dúró bí wọ́n ṣe rí olùkọ́
The pupils stood when they saw the teacher.
Akẹ́kọ̀ọ́: Ẹ káàsán, olùkọ́. Good afternoon, teacher.
Olùkọ́: Ẹ káàsán o, ẹ̀yin akẹ́kọ̀ọ́.
Ṣé dáadáa ni?		 Good afternoon, students.
Hope you are fine?
Akẹ́kọ̀ọ́: Dáadáa ni olùkọ́. We are fine, teacher.
Olùkọ́: Ẹ ṣeun o. Ẹ jókòó. Thank you. Have your seat.
Convers V:
Ìkíni láàrin Délé àti Bàbá Rẹ̀
Greetings between Délé and His Father
Délé dọ̀bálẹ̀ kí bàbá rẹ̀.
Délé prostrated to greet his father.
Délé: Ẹ káàsán o, baba.. Good afternoon, father
Bàbá: Káàsán o, Délé.
Ṣé dáadáa ni?		 Good afternoon, Délé.
Hope you are fine?
Délé: Dáadáa ni, a dúpẹ́. Fine, thank you.

Ìrọ̀lẹ́
Evening

Convers VI:
Ìkíni láàrin ọkùnrin méjì
Greetings between two men
Ọkùnrin méjì pàdé ara wọn lẹ̀yin iṣẹ́.
Two men met on their way from work
Ọkùnrin 1: Ẹ kú ìrọ̀lẹ́ o. Good evening.
Ọkùnrin 2: Ooo! Yes!
Ọkùnrin 1: Ẹ kú àtàárọ̀.
Báwo ni iṣẹ́ lónìí? 		Trust your day is fine.
How is work?
Ọkùnrin 2: Dáadáa ni o, a dúpẹ́. Fine, thank you.

Alẹ́
Late Evening

Convers VII:
Ìkíni láàrin Bímpé àti Ìyá Rẹ̀
Greetings between Bímpé and Her Mother
Bímpé kúnlẹ̀ kí ìyá rẹ̀.
Bímpé knelt to greet her mother.
Bímpé: Ẹ káalẹ́ o, ìyá. Good evening, mother.
Ìyá: Káalẹ́ o, ọmọ mi.
Ṣé dáadáa ni? 		Good evening, my daughter.
Hope you are fine?
Bímpé: A dúpẹ́, mà. Thank you, ma.

Instructions

To get the best out of the lessons, carefully:


1. Listen to the voice 2. Read the text (first) in Yorùbá and then in English 3. Read the Yorùbá text along with the voice 4. Repeat after the voice.
For greetings, you may share the characters between another learner
NB!
Do this repeatedly until you feel comfortable you've mastered the conversation (one at a time, please).

Immitate the voice. This puts your mouth through the conversations/exercises and helps you to pronounce the words/phrases/sentances in good Yorùbá (with correct tones).
The more you repeat, the more you get familiar with the content and language in use.
